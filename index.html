About Me

Explaining my analytics journey:

Heading into college as an undeclared, I didn't really know what I wanted to do. All I knew was that I liked ecology and I liked math, so naturally I figured I'd aim to major in environmental engineering. After trying some of the required coursework, I realized I wasn't as into it as I thought I'd be. As a result, I opted to major in Ecology instead. Graduating from college, I still wasn't really sure what I wanted to do. I wasn't sold on the idea of going for my masters and seeking out a career centered around ecology-related research, and I honestly wasn't really sure what an Ecology degree was good for beyond that. At the end of the day, I didn't really gain any practical skills, all I had was knowledge. In any case, life had to go on, so I chose to work at the San Diego Zoo thinking I could strive for working with animals or perhaps something animal research related. I also wasn't sold on that idea but at some point you just have to choose (as I said, life goes on). As time progressed, I became less and less convinced that a future with animals was what I wanted, so I reached out to one of my old math teachers from high school, with whom I had gotten along fairly well. Him, knowing my character better than I did, said that he really thought I could do well with a career in data analytics. I knew nothing of data analytics at that point, so I did my research into it and every math-related career, and I also agreed that data analytics seemed like something I could really enjoy. After all, I like story-telling, I like math, I'm a bit artsy, and I have a very inquisitive/analytical mind. The coding I wasn't so sure about, but I was willing to give it a shot. I eventually opted to enroll in a virtual data analytics program provided by Chegg Skills/Thinkful and took it upon myself to continue learning after its completion. The rest is history.
